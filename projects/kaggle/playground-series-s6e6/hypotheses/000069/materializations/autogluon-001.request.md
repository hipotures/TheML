# Hypothesis
- title: Scan-order photometric incoherence
- group_name: sdss_scan_order_incoherence
- family: temporal_photometry
- summary: Represent whether an object's five-band photometry is smoother in physical wavelength order than in the SDSS camera scan-time order, exposing non-simultaneous, moving-source, variability, or band-quality effects that ordinary color features can miss.
- strategy: Use the documented SDSS imaging order r,i,u,z,g with 71.7 second filter spacing and 54 second exposures (https://www.sdss3.org/dr9/imaging/imaging_basics.php, https://www.sdss4.org/instruments/camera/). Convert u,g,r,i,z magnitudes to relative log-flux after subtracting the per-row median magnitude, fit a robust low-order continuum versus log central wavelength using the five SDSS effective wavelengths, and compute residuals. Reorder those residuals into scan order r,i,u,z,g and derive scan-order first differences, second differences, maximum absolute temporal jump, signed early-minus-late residual contrast, temporal roughness divided by wavelength-order roughness, scan-order residual autocorrelation, and the largest residual jump across the non-wavelength-adjacent scan steps i-to-u and z-to-g. Normalize all roughness and jump quantities by the row's total residual absolute deviation plus a small epsilon; clip extreme magnitudes and residual ratios at robust finite bounds; if the continuum fit is degenerate, fall back to median-centered residuals.
- expected_signal: Stars, galaxies, and quasars can share similar broad colors, but point-source variability and SDSS non-simultaneous imaging are known to help quasar-related selection in multi-epoch settings (https://arxiv.org/abs/1508.04121, https://www.sdss4.org/dr17/algorithms/ancillary/boss/variable225/), while scan-order residual structure may also flag moving-source or band-specific measurement artifacts that affect stellar-like objects differently from extended galaxy-like objects.
- risk: The 71.7 second SDSS inter-filter spacing is much shorter than most quasar variability timescales, so the signal may be weak and partly redundant with single-band anomaly or SED-roughness features; scan-order artifacts may also be survey-processing-specific and unstable if the synthetic data do not preserve the original SDSS acquisition geometry.
