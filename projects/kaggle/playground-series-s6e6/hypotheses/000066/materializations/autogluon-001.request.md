- group_name: spectroscopic_redshift_grid_imprint
- family: spectroscopic_pipeline_geometry
- summary: Represent each object by how its reported redshift sits within the numerical search windows and velocity-grid structure used by SDSS-style spectral template classification, capturing pipeline-imprinted evidence separate from raw redshift magnitude.
- strategy: Using only redshift, compute a clipped z value with lower bound -0.999 for stable log and velocity transforms; derive ln(1+z), relativistic beta=((1+z)^2-1)/((1+z)^2+1), velocity_kms=299792.458*beta, and log_velocity_kms=299792.458*ln(1+z). For the documented SDSS trial spacings of 138 km/s for galaxy fits and 276 km/s for quasar fits, compute signed nearest-grid residuals log_velocity_kms - step*round(log_velocity_kms/step), absolute residuals divided by half-step, and sin/cos phase on each grid. Encode template-search-window geometry as signed normalized margins and inside indicators for stellar radial velocity abs(velocity_kms)<=1200, galaxy redshift -0.01<=z<=1.00, and quasar redshift 0.0333<=z<=7.00; additionally encode the count and categorical pattern of these memberships and the clipped signed distance to their shared boundaries. Clip extreme normalized distances to a finite range such as [-5,5], keep all operations deterministic, and add no label-derived statistics. Source basis: https://www.sdss3.org/dr10/algorithms/redshifts.php
- expected_signal: The target labels are tied to spectroscopic template families whose redshift solutions live in different velocity ranges and search windows, so near-zero stellar velocities, low-redshift galaxy solutions, quasar-only high-redshift regions, and ambiguous overlap zones may become clearer than from raw redshift alone.
- risk: Much of the useful information may already be captured by redshift, and the grid-phase features may be weak because final SDSS redshifts are locally refined; the group is also survey-pipeline-specific and could overfit numerical conventions if test data were produced differently.
