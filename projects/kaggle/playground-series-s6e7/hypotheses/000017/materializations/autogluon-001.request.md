- title: Weight Status Activity Buffer
- group_name: weight_status_activity_buffer
- family: body_context_buffer
- summary: Represent whether non-ideal body-mass status is behaviorally buffered or amplified by observed movement, separating weight-related risk that occurs alongside active habits from weight-related risk paired with sedentary behavior.
- strategy: Create a deterministic body-mass zone from BMI using CDC adult cut points: unknown, underweight <18.5, healthy 18.5 to <25, overweight 25 to <30, and obesity >=30; compute body-mass severity as distance outside the healthy band, scaled by 5 BMI units and clipped to 0-2, with missing BMI kept as unknown. Build an activity support ladder from available movement evidence: step_count bins <5000, 5000-7499, 7500-9999, 10000-12499, and >=12500 mapped to -2,-1,0,1,2; exercise_duration bins 0, >0 to <15, 15 to <30, and >=30 mapped to -1,0,1,2; physical_activity_level sedentary, moderate, active mapped to -1,0,1; average only observed components and assign an unknown activity state if all are missing. Emit the BMI zone ordinal, activity support score/state, non-healthy-BMI flag, amplified weight risk as body-mass severity times the sedentary side of activity support, buffered weight risk as body-mass severity times the active side, healthy-weight inactivity as healthy BMI times the sedentary side, and a compact crossed category combining BMI zone with low/neutral/high activity. Use no target labels, no identifier logic, and no train/test-wide learned statistics. Domain anchors: CDC BMI categories https://www.cdc.gov/bmi/adult-calculator/bmi-categories.html, CDC adult activity guidance https://www.cdc.gov/physical-activity-basics/guidelines/adults.html, and the step-defined activity index https://link.springer.com/article/10.1186/1479-5868-8-79.
- expected_signal: The target may distinguish students whose body-mass risk is accompanied by insufficient activity from students with similar BMI but stronger protective behavior, which can clarify fit versus at-risk versus unhealthy labels beyond raw BMI or raw movement levels alone and help balanced accuracy on minority classes.
- risk: General adult thresholds may not match the synthetic label generator or the student population exactly, and the group can be redundant with existing guideline-deviation or activity-composition signals if the target treats BMI and activity additively rather than as contextual compensation.
