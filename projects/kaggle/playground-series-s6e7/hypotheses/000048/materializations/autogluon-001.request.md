# Hypothesis
- title: Sedentary Offset Balance
- group_name: sedentary_offset_balance
- family: activity_sedentary_context
- summary: Represent whether a student's explicit and incidental movement appears sufficient to offset a sedentary waking-day context, treating sedentary exposure as partly independent from exercise volume.
- strategy: Use the physical-activity/sedentary distinction supported by WHO and AHA guidance (https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7719906/ and https://www.heart.org/en/healthy-living/exercise-and-physical-activity/fitness-basics/aha-recs-for-physical-activity-in-adults): compute estimated waking hours as 24 minus sleep duration clipped to a plausible range, step density as steps per waking hour, exercise sufficiency as exercise duration divided by a 30-minute daily reference and clipped to [0,2], incidental movement sufficiency as step_count divided by 7500 and clipped to [0,2], activity-label support from physical_activity_level mapped sedentary=0, moderate=0.5, active=1, and calorie support from calorie_expenditure train-feature percentile rank clipped to [0,1]. Build a sedentary pressure score from sedentary label, low step density, low calorie support, and long waking time; build an offset score from exercise sufficiency, incidental movement sufficiency, and activity-label support; derive offset gap = sedentary pressure minus offset score, an offset quadrant code for high/low sedentary pressure crossed with high/low offset, and a concentrated-exercise flag when exercise sufficiency is high but incidental movement sufficiency and activity-label support are low. Treat missing components as neutral 0.5 and average only observed terms where possible, with all outputs clipped to stable finite ranges.
- expected_signal: Sedentary behavior can remain adverse even when moderate-to-vigorous activity is present (see https://journals.plos.org/plosone/article?id=10.1371/journal.pone.0037696), so this group may help separate fit-like active movers from at-risk students whose exercise is concentrated or insufficient to counter a sedentary daily pattern, improving class balance around the at-risk boundary.
- risk: This may overlap with existing activity-composition features, and fixed daily exercise or step references could be unstable if exercise_duration represents a different reporting period or if physical_activity_level already encodes the same sedentary-offset concept.
